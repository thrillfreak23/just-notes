# Writing

Will you come to the aid of the sentients who subsist in the
Low-Canals Region of Wâhaz? Will you pray for them... 

Where the winds howl and scrape with abrasive dust of heavy metals.
Where the spaceships make planetfall and Low-Canal women called
greeters or *salvetators* in the truly vulgar Latin of the region say
*Salve* when you debark and it's the only Latin they know. Where
nightrunners thieve the contents of larders and cradles and leave the
front door swinging as they are gone into the darkling waste. The
native homin gather in heaps of hovels they are pleased to call towns,
cities. Manufactoria glow and burn and smoke day and night, in
subarctic cold and summer warmth. And through the long cool Spring of
the year and the long cool autumn too the latifundia are crawling with
figures stooping to the red earth doing all the work. The people march
homeward like armies of irregulars utterly spent from some universal
cataclysm of the world. The huge sails of the iceboats dart like
brightfeathered birds down the wide and frozen canals that converge
here, they sail straight as the light of a holophote and gathering
astounding speed, the only thing visible that is not dreary, as they
vanish with distance around the foreshortened curvature of this small
world. Flat terrain of red dust maculated with green, low cities of
red and grey brick, small hills that disappear when the air gets
dusty, and scrannel trees looking metallic and skeletal standing
against the cold gails but trembling as they do so. The people turn to
subspace radio dramas and drugs and vox stations and sex and a
religion old as the homin species for stimulation and escape, and all
are available for just a little money. Escape is imperative here,
especially when leaving is impossible.

See the man, he is dressed in black leather like a spacer, like he
just made planetfall and stepped straight into this tavern or bar. The
poisonous climates of hot and alien worlds seem to reek and threaten
from his very clothing. His cheeks are hollow, his eyes crazed and
bright and empty, and he is drinking heavily and without pleasure.
Next to him is a heavier unit, a homin female burlier than him (and he
came from Skâr by the look of him, also judging from where we *are*,
mythical old Yrth itself is the next planet away!) -- not a pretty
woman next to him, not gracile like a Wâhazene. Next to *her* is a
starved addict, actually a Wâhazene to judge by her appearance, as we
seem wont to do here. She is snorting *foifir* from an iridium mirror
laid upon the wooden table. She raises herself up, red hair flying.
--"That's good, that's better," she says and wipes her lovely,
trifling nose.

... now a gentleman who is surely a scholar in the local academe is
growing drunken and prolix on the subject of the latest Date-of-Easter
controversy, a large and Gordian affair considering that here on Wâhaz
it's based on the motions in outerspace of an alien world and how best
to translate them onto the surface and into the minds of the faithful
of Wâhaz, and considering also that no matter the final decision few
will care and those who still object to it will be implacable in their
hatred of the teaching for the rest of their lives. So says the
gentleman scholar. It is not occuring to him that these people at the
next table -- the man, a spacer by the look of him, and the two women
-- probably do not attend Mass at any Holy Martian Catholic Church.

A lanky barbarian nearby got his feelings hurt, no one knew how. No
one knew him. But the gentleman scholar went home with a concussion
and a bloody scalp. No doubt he thanked his God, because the man in
black leather lay supine upon the floor, perfectly still with a
steaming holophote hole in his chest. The barbarian had his gun taken
away and was led off in chains by a magistrate and his cataphract. No
doubt he contemplated a lifetime of forced labor, tilling the earth of
some latifundium and sweating in the summer and shivering in the
hateful winter, and with no choice at all forever.  

The burly Skârkin woman had vanished, leaving only the gracile
Wâhazkin female, in other words *me*. I'm the one who looked like a
native *foifir* addict, because at that time I was one. My policy in
life, my personal philosophy, my personal *mythlogy*, demands that I let
it all out. Hide nothing. Tell all. This is my story.
